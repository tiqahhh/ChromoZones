📚 Detailed Stage Explanations

🔹 Meiosis I: Reduction Division

Purpose: Reduces chromosome number from diploid (2n) to haploid (n)

Prophase I:

Chromosomes condense, homologous chromosomes pair (synapsis), crossing over occurs, spindle forms

Metaphase I:

Homologous chromosome pairs align at the metaphase plate (equator of cell)

Anaphase I:

Homologous chromosomes separate and move to opposite poles

Telophase I & Cytokinesis:

Cell divides into two haploid cells, each containing one chromosome from each homologous pair

🔹 Meiosis II: Similar to Mitosis

Purpose: Separates sister chromatids to form gametes

Prophase II:

Chromosomes condense again, spindle forms in each haploid cell

Metaphase II:

Chromosomes align at the metaphase plate

Anaphase II:

Sister chromatids separate and move to opposite poles

Telophase II & Cytokinesis:

Four unique haploid cells (gametes) are produced

🌟 Sources of Genetic Variation in Meiosis

1. Crossing Over (Recombination): Exchange of genetic material between homologous chromosomes during Prophase I, creating new combinations of alleles

2. Independent Assortment: Random distribution of homologous chromosomes during Anaphase I results in 2^n possible combinations

Example: Humans have 23 chromosome pairs, creating 2^23 = 8.4 million possible combinations!

🎯 Key Concepts to Remember

Diploid to Haploid

Meiosis I reduces chromosome number from 2n to n, producing two haploid cells

Four Gametes

Complete meiosis produces four unique haploid gametes from one diploid cell

Genetic Diversity

Crossing over and independent assortment ensure each gamete is genetically unique

Two Divisions

Meiosis I & II must both occur for complete cell division and gamete formation
