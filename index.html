Quick Overview

What is Mitosis?

  • Mitosis is the process where one cell divides to form two identical cells.

  • Each new cell has the same DNA and number of chromosomes as the parent cell.

  • This process is important for growth, repair, and replacing damaged cells in the body.

  • Mitosis happens in body cells and goes through four main stages:

    • 1. Prophase (P)

    2. Metaphase (M)

    3. Anaphase (A)

    4. Telophase (T)

What is Meiosis?

  • Meiosis is the process where one cell divides to form four non-identical cells.

  • Each cell has half the number of chromosomes of the parent cell.

  • This process is important for sexual reproduction because it produces gametes (sperm and egg cells).

  • Meiosis happens in two stages which help create genetic variation:

    • 1. Meiosis I (Reduction Division)

    Prophase I, Metaphase I, Anaphase I and Telophase I

    2. Meiosis II (Equational Division)

    Prophase II, Metaphase II, Anaphase II and Telophase II
