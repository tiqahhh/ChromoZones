PHASE-BY-PHASE BREAKDOWN

THE MEIOSIS I PROCESS

📢 Scroll down slowly to observe and discover the different phases of meiosis.

▶ Prophase I

♦️Chromatin shortens and thickens to form visible chromosomes.
♦️Homologous chromosomes pair up in a process called synapsis.
♦️Each pair forms a bivalent (tetrad) which contains four chromatids.
♦️Crossing over occurs, where genetic material is exchanged between non-identical chromatids.(This process produces new gene combinations.)
♦️The point where crossing over occurs is called chiasma.
♦️Nuclear membrane and nucleolus begin to disappear.
♦️Centrioles move to opposite poles of the cell.
♦️Spindle fibres start to form.

▶ Metaphase I

♦️Homologous chromosome pairs align at the equatorial plane (middle of the cell).
♦️One chromosome of each pair attaches to spindle fibres from one pole.
♦️The homologous chromosome attaches to spindle fibres from the opposite pole.
♦️Sister chromatids remain joined because the centromere has not separated.

▶ Anaphase I

♦️Spindle fibres contract and pull homologous chromosomes apart.
♦️Each homologous chromosome moves to opposite poles of the cell.
♦️Each chromosome still consists of two sister chromatids joined at the centromere.

▶ Telophase I

♦️Chromosomes arrive at opposite poles of the cell.
♦️Each pole contains haploid chromosomes (one set only).
♦️Spindle fibres disappear.
♦️Nucleolus reappears and nuclear membrane reforms.
♦️Cytokinesis occurs, producing two haploid daughter cells.
♦️The interphase between Meiosis I and II is short and DNA does not replicate again.

THE MEIOSIS II PROCESS

▶ Prophase II

♦️Nuclear membrane and nucleolus disappear.
♦️Each chromosome still consists of two sister chromatids joined at the centromere.
♦️Spindle fibres begin to form in both daughter cells.

▶ Metaphase II

♦️Chromosomes align randomly at the equatorial plane.
♦️Each chromatid attaches to spindle fibres at the centromere.
♦️The stage ends when the centromeres separate.

▶ Anaphase II

♦️Centromeres split.
♦️Sister chromatids separate and move to opposite poles.
♦️Each separated chromatid is now called a chromosome.

▶ Telophase II

♦️Chromosomes arrive at the poles of the cell.
♦️Spindle fibres disappear.
♦️Nuclear membranes and nucleoli reform.
♦️Cytokinesis occurs, producing four haploid daughter cells.
♦️Each haploid cell:
↪ Has half the number of chromosomes of the parent cell.
↪ Has different genetic information.
↪ Develops into gametes (sex cells).
