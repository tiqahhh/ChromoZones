Mitosis vs Meiosis

▶ Mitosis and Meiosis are both cell division processes which serve different purposes and have important differences. Let's learn it!


THE DIFFERENCES BETWEEN MITOSIS AND MEIOSIS
ASPECT MITOSIS MEIOSIS
Purpose Growth, repair, and asexual reproduction of body cells Production of gametes for sexual reproduction
Location Occurs in somatic (body) cells Occurs in reproductive organs (germ cells)
Number of Divisions One division Two divisions (Meiosis I & II)
Daughter Cells Produced Two identical diploid cells (2n) Four non-identical haploid cells (n)
Chromosome Number Remains the same (2n → 2n) Reduced to half (2n → n)
Genetic Identity Daughter cells are genetically identical All four cells are genetically unique
Homologous Pairing No pairing occurs. Yes.It happened during synapsis in Prophase I
Crossing Over No crossing over Yes. It occurs in Prophase I.
Independent Assortment No. Chromosomes do not separated by homolog. Yes. Random distribution of homologs occurs in Anaphase I.
Genetic Variation The events do not create genetic variation, only clones produced The events creates genetic diversity which is essential for evolution
Sister Chromatid Separation Occurs in Anaphase Occurs in Anaphase II
Result in Humans Two identical somatic cells Four gametes
• In males : Sperms
• In females : An egg with 3 polar bodies
